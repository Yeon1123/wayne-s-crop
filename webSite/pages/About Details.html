About Details

We made an irrigation system with our sensor and survey data. Below we will break down and explain the components of this system.

The sensor gave us temperature and humidity data. The sensor communicates through the LoRa gateway and updates our data every 15 seconds as seen on the “View Data” page. We then compare this data with optimal temperature and humidity levels obtained from nongsaro.go and display this on our “Eco System” page. If the humidity is lower than the optimal level; the water pump will irrigate our plants for 10 minutes. After running for 10 minutes the pump will pause for 24 hours to let the soil absorb the moisture. When there is no need for irrigation, for example, a harvest or heavy rain, the system can be disabled in the “Settings” page. This can make farming more effective and use less water.

We want to compare our irrigation data against optimal levels of moisture and climate data to see if there is a noticeable improvement in crop irrigation using our system. Standard systems do not monitor the moisture content of the soil and will apply the same amount of water to a dry area as to a wet area. This is wasteful and what our system hopes to alleviate.

We analyzed 9 years'(2010 ~ 2018) of climate data in Indianapolis. I did a randomized block design and one-way anova design to see if it is effective and eco-friendly. The bottom of the page shows the analyzing result.

Conclusion

In light of the two results, the total amount of water received by the crops is different when the water is not given separately, when a person is given water according to the manual, and when the irrigation system is given water in consideration of temperature and humidity. When watered by and when the irrigation system is used, we can see that the irrigation system statistically reduces water waste, which means that our irrigation system can save agricultural water.
