Indianapolis' average annual Weather

In Indianapolis, summer is long, warm, humid and rainy, winter is very cold and windy; and partly cloudy throughout the year. Throughout the year, the temperature typically changes from -6°C to 30°C, and rarely below -16°C or above.


Temperature

The hot weather lasts 3.9 months from May 23 to September 21, and the average daily temperature is above 24°C. The hottest day of the year is July 19, with an average high temperature of 30°C and a low temperature of 20°C.

The cold weather lasts 3.0 months from December 1st to March 1st, and the average daily temperature is below 8°C. The coldest day of the year is January 29, with an average temperature of -6°C and a high temperature of 2°C.

Rainfall

It's not just the total of the moon, but also the amount of rain accumulated over the 31-day sliding period around each day, to show the change during the month. Indianapolis has a significant change in rainfall every month.

It rains throughout the year in Indianapolis. The heaviest rainfall during 31 months is around May 25, with an average total accumulation of 106 millimeters.

The lowest rainfall period is around January 28, with an average total accumulation of 41 millimeters.




Humidity

Because water cools down when it evaporates from the skin, the dew point determines when evaporation occurs is based on humidity stability. Lower dew points make it drier and higher dew points feel more humid. Unlike the temperature that varies significantly between night and day, the dew point changes more slowly, so even if the temperature falls at night, it becomes a hot, humid, low, warm night.

Indianapolis has extreme perceived moisture changes.

The warmer, warmer period of the day lasts 3.9 months from May 25 to September 22, and the stability during the period is very hot and humid, and 14% are very hot and humid. The hottest day of the year is July 22 and the hottest day of the year is 56%.

The least hot day of the year is December 23, and it's never.




Growing season

The definition of growth period differs throughout the world, but for the purposes of this report, it is defined as the longest period of non-condensing temperature ( 0 0°C) continuously during the year (reversal year in the Northern Hemisphere or from July 1 to June 30 in the Southern Hemisphere).

Indianapolis' growth season typically lasts for 6.5 months, from around April 8 to around October 26, hardly begins before March 23 or after April 25, and rarely ends before October 4 or after November 15.

A growth temperature day is a measure of accumulated annual heat used in plant and animal adults, defined as an integer of warmth above the basic temperature, except for the maximum temperature. In this report, the best use is 30°C based on 10°C.

By default, Indianapolis' first spring flowers appear around March 29 and are rarely seen before March 13 or after April 14.
